Abstract#==#Task 18 at SemEval 2015 defines Broad-Coverage Semantic Dependency Parsing
(SDP) as the problem of recovering sentence-internal predicate–argument
relationships for all content words, i.e. the semantic structure constituting
the relational core of sentence meaning.  In this task description, we position
the problem in comparison to other language analysis sub-tasks, introduce and
compare the semantic dependency target representations used, and summarize the
task setup, participating systems, and main results.
