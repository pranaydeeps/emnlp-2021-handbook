Abstract#==#In Semantic Textual Similarity, systems rate the degree of
semantic equivalence, on a graded scale from 0 to 5, with 5 being the
most similar. For the English subtask, we present a system which
relies on several resources for token-to-token and phrase-to-phrase
similarity to build a data-structure which holds all the information,
and then combine the information to get a similarity score. We also
participated in the pilot on Interpretable STS, where we apply a
pipeline which first aligns tokens, then chunks, and then uses
classifier to label each chunk alignment.
