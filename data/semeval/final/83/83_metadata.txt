Abstract#==#This paper describes MITRE’s participation in the Paraphrase and Semantic
Similarity in Twitter task (SemEval-2015 Task 1). This effort placed first in
Semantic Similarity and second in Paraphrase Identification with scores of
Pearson’s r of 61.9%, F1 of 66.7%, and maxF1 of 72.4%. We detail the
approaches we explored including mixtures of string matching metrics,
alignments using tweet-specific distributed word representations, recurrent
neural networks for modeling similarity with those alignments, and distance
measurements on pooled latent semantic features. Logistic regression is used to
tie the systems together into the ensembles submitted for evaluation.
